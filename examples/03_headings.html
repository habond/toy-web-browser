Heading Levels Demonstration

This example shows all six heading levels and their relative sizes.

Heading Level 1 (h1) - Largest

H1 headings are typically used for main page titles. They use a 2.0x multiplier.

Heading Level 2 (h2) - Large

H2 headings are used for major sections. They use a 1.75x multiplier.

Heading Level 3 (h3) - Medium-Large

H3 headings are used for subsections. They use a 1.5x multiplier.

Heading Level 4 (h4) - Medium

H4 headings are used for sub-subsections. They use a 1.25x multiplier.

Heading Level 5 (h5) - Small

H5 headings are used for minor sections. They use a 1.1x multiplier.

Heading Level 6 (h6) - Smallest

H6 headings are the smallest heading level. They use a 1.0x multiplier (same as body text).
